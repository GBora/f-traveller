Grand Duchy Knight

Description

The martial class of the Grand Duchy, the Knight represents the backbone of the state, being at the same time the core of their armies and the landowners who oversee the majority peasant populance.

While officials of the state that are not members of one of the Knightly Orders exist there are far and few in between their positions of power being granted only due to their unique skills (commerce, magic, religion etc)

Besides these exceptions the Knights represent the Grand Duchy in all matters, wether as the army fighting any external foes or in their own lands crusading to clear any monsters that would threathen the peasantry or acting as patrons for the building of new roads, churches or markets.

Requirements

Faction Relationship: Grand Duchy Favored+

To be a kight of the Grand Duchy is to wield a huge ammount of power and influence, both military power and civil one thus the title is only awarded to the sons and daughters of the Grand Duchy and on very rare cases to those foreigners that have shown themselves as extraordinary heroes and friends of the Grand Duchy.

Attribute: Str 6+

A Knight is a warrior, defending his country on the field of battle wether mounted or on foot, to fall short when battle is met is to not be worthy of the title and to have it stripped from you.

Qualification Roll: Soc 7+

To be given the opportunity of induction into one of the Grand Duchies's Knightly Orders requires that a individual be able to make a supremely good impression on the members of the order, or to be born Grand Duchy Nobility.

Material Qualifications

A prospective knight must assemble the following equipment out of his own purse before being considered for knighthood:

  1. 1 warhorse
  2. 1 complete set of metal armour
  3. 1 melee weapon

Unfortunate Events
Roll 1d6 Description
1 You get injured in combat, roll on the Injury Table.
2 Your division gets deployed to a border skirmish with the Duchy, one of their knights feels personally slighted by your conduct, you gain a Enemy.
3 You get assigned to put down a revolt in one of the Empire's provinces, roll either Persuade or Leadership if you fail decrease Soc by 1 and gain a Enemy.
4 Your commanding officer does not see you favourably due to his report you get a -4 DM to your next Advancement roll.
5 You are blamed by a senior mage for a complex ritual failing, as a punishment the next time you would be awarded a new spell, you will be denied.
6 You are injured lightly in combat, you need to recover but there are no obvious side effects.
Opportunities
1d6 Roll Description
1 You lead your troops in raiding a local bandit lair, get 1 roll on the artefact table.
2 You remove a curse from a important member of the community your troops are stationed at, gain 1 Ally.
3 You see action defending the border from invaders, either increase End by 1 or learn a new spell.
4 Your unit is sent to conquer the temple of a demon summoning cult you gain 2 spells from looting their archives.
5 You impress your mentor with your spellwork on the battlefield, you gain a +4 DM to your next advancement roll.
6 You take the initiative and lead your unit into an assault on the enemy general, forcing him to flee you automatically succeed your next advancement roll.
