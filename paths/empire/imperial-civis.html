Imperial Civis

Description

Citizens of the Empire, and the ones that ultimately are to praise or blame for it's success, they farm the land, work the forges, staff it's libraries and provide the raw conscripts for it's infamous Legions.

While the emperors and senators think of themselves as sallying forth and conquering or civilizing the world in the name of the Empire, there isn't a fort or road built, a statue erected or sword forged without one of the common people putting their back to it.

Having different skill sets whether they work with their hands or with their wisdom and whether they hail from one of the Empire's huge cities or one of it's new colonies, the civis display abilities that make them the envy of all other kingdoms and the builders of the civilised world.

Requirements

Faction Relationship: Empire Neutral

As they are the citizens of the Empire, the civis are expect to at least nominally follow it's rules and be in good standing with the Imperial Government, the local officials might not keep track of the day to the life of every countryman but high crimes and betrayals can easily lead to all rights and privileges being thrown aside as the Empire no longer considers you a citizen

Attribute: End 5+

Surviving in the empire is easier than in some other human nations but it is no easy task, one must be ready to toil either with the body or the mind.

Qualification Roll: None/Soc 5+

If born in the Empire you are automatically considered a citizen, in the cases that one wants to become a citizen or needs to due to their lands being conquered or colonised by the Empire, they can swear an Oath of Allegiance and renounce their old citizenship they will then be tested by the agents of the local governor and depending on the result be admitted or not.

Specializations
Colonist Conquerors of raw nature, they bring civilization to nature and their frontiersman skills to civilization.
Scholar Seekers of wisdom, they define Imperial culture and keep track of it's values, riches and magic all the while trying to expand them.
Polis Workers from the Empire's vast cities, they are the lifeblood of the Empire no sword is forged, good exchanged or book written to paper without them.

Unfortunate Events
Roll 1d6 Description
1 You get attacked by bandits on the road, roll on the Injury Table.
2 You catch a thief trying to steal from your house, he escapes but decides that you know too much you gain a Enemy.
3 You are sent as part of a diplomatic party to increase trade relations with another region, roll Soc or Diplomacy if you fail you lose one roll on the Wealth table.
4 Your guild master does not approve of your efforts, you get a -4 DM to your next Advancement roll.
5 You are blamed for loosing a trade oportunity, lose one roll on the Wealth table.
6 You are injured lightly in tavern brawl, y ou need to recover but there are no obvious side effects.
Opportunities
1d6 Roll Description
1 You have a good trade season gain one roll on the Wealth table.
2 An artifact trader passes through your community and remembers a debt he owes to your family you gain one roll on the Artifacts table.
3 You enroll in the local militia supporting the Legion and being trained by them, increase either End or Str by 1.
4 Your community begins trade relations with a newly discovered Barbarian tribe and you are instrumental in forging the alliance, gain two rolls on the Wealth table and two Allies.
5 You manage to uncover an enemy spy which was trying to inflitrate the province, the local governor takes note and you gain a +4 DM to your next advancement roll.
6 You take initiative in rallying your fellow civis in fending off raiders, when the Legions arrive the commander personally takes note of your courage making your next advancement roll an automatic success.
